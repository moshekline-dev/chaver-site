The Co-Living Concept

Unlike traditional apartment buildings or institutional student dormitories, premium boutique co-living offers the ideal balance: private sanctuary combined with vibrant community.

Each resident enjoys a fully private ensuite bedroom with personal bathroom—their own locked space for rest, study, and privacy. But beyond their door lies something far more valuable: genuine community. Understanding the 20% IRR

Internal Rate of Return (IRR) is the gold standard metric for measuring investment performance. It accounts for all cash flows—initial investment, annual returns, and final exit—and expresses them as an annualized percentage return.

What IRR Actually Means

IRR is the annual interest rate that makes the Net Present Value of all cash flows equal to zero.

In plain English: If you invested €963,326 in a savings account earning exactly 20.1% per year for 10 years with annual withdrawals matching this project's cash flows (including the Year 4 refinancing), you would end up with the same final value.

The IRR Formula

IRR solves for the discount rate (r) in the Net Present Value equation:

0 = -€963,326 + Year 4 Refinance/(1+r)^4 + Σ (Annual Cash Flows) / (1+r)^n + Exit Value / (1+r)^10

Solving this equation yields: r = 20.1% per year
