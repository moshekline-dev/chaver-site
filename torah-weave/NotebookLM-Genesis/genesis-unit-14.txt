And in thee and in thy seed shall all the families of the earth be blessed. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>28:15</b></span> And, behold, I am with thee, and will keep thee whithersoever thou goest, and will bring thee back into this land; for I will not leave thee, until I have done that which I have spoken to thee of.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>28:16</b></span> And Jacob awaked out of his sleep, and he said: 'Surely the LORD is in this place; and I knew it not.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>28:17</b></span> And he was afraid, and said: 'How full of awe is this place! this is none other than the house of God, and this is the gate of heaven.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>28:18</b></span> And Jacob rose up early in the morning, and took the stone that he had put under his head, and set it up for a pillar, and poured oil upon the top of it. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>28:19</b></span> <span class="closure"><b>And he called the name of that place</b></span> Beth-el, but the name of the city was Luz at the first. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>28:20</b></span> And Jacob vowed a vow, saying: 'If God will be with me, and will keep me in this way that I go, and will give me bread to eat, and raiment to put on, <span class="VerseNumber"><b>28:21</b></span> so that I come back to my father's house in peace, then shall the LORD be my God, <span class="VerseNumber"><b>28:22</b></span> and this stone, which I have set up for a pillar, shall be God's house; and of all that Thou shalt give me I will surely give the tenth unto Thee.'</p>

                                <p class="torah"><span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:1</b></span> Then Jacob went on his journey, and came to the land of the children of the east. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:2</b></span> <span class="horizontal1"><b>And he looked</b></span>, and behold a well in the field, and, lo, three flocks of sheep lying there by it.--For out of that well they watered the flocks. And the stone upon the well's mouth was great. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:3</b></span> And thither were all the flocks gathered; and they rolled the stone from the well's mouth, and watered the sheep, and put the stone back upon the well's mouth in its place.-- <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:4</b></span> And Jacob said unto them: 'My brethren, whence are ye?' And they said: 'Of Haran are we.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:5</b></span> And he said unto them: 'Know ye Laban the son of Nahor?' And they said: 'We know him.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:6</b></span> And he said unto them: 'Is it well with him?' And they said: 'It is well; and, behold, Rachel his daughter cometh with the sheep.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:7</b></span> And he said: 'Lo, it is yet high day, neither is it time that the cattle should be gathered together; water ye the sheep, and go and feed them.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:8</b></span> And they said: 'We cannot, until all the flocks be gathered together, and they roll the stone from the well's mouth; then we water the sheep.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:9</b></span> While he was yet speaking with them, Rachel came with her father's sheep; for she tended them. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:10</b></span> And it came to pass, when Jacob saw Rachel the daughter of Laban his mother's brother, and the sheep of Laban his mother's brother, that Jacob went near, and rolled the stone from the well's mouth, and watered the flock of Laban his mother's brother. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:11</b></span> And Jacob kissed Rachel, and lifted up his voice, and wept. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:12</b></span> And Jacob told Rachel that he was her father's brother, and that he was Rebekah's son; and she ran and told her father. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:13</b></span> And it came to pass, when Laban heard the tidings of Jacob his sister's son, that he ran to meet him, and embraced him, and kissed him, and brought him to his house. And he told Laban all these things. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:14</b></span> And Laban said to him: 'Surely thou art my bone and my flesh.' And he abode with him the space of a month. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:15</b></span> And Laban said unto Jacob: 'Because thou art my brother, shouldest thou therefore serve me for nought? tell me, what shall thy wages be?' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:16</b></span> Now Laban had two daughters: the name of the elder was Leah, and the name of the younger was Rachel. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:17</b></span> And Leah's eyes were weak; but Rachel was of beautiful form and fair to look upon. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:18</b></span> And Jacob loved Rachel; and he said: 'I will serve thee seven years for Rachel thy younger daughter.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:19</b></span> And Laban said: 'It is better that I give her to thee, than that I should give her to another man; abide with me.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:20</b></span> And Jacob served seven years for Rachel; and they seemed unto him but a few days, for the love he had to her. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:21</b></span> And Jacob said unto Laban: 'Give me my wife, for my days are filled, that I may go in unto her.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:22</b></span> And Laban gathered together all the men of the place, and made a feast. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:23</b></span> And it came to pass in the evening, that he took Leah his daughter, and brought her to him; and he went in unto her. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:24</b></span> And Laban gave Zilpah his handmaid unto his daughter Leah for a handmaid. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:25</b></span> And it came to pass in the morning that, behold, it was Leah; and he said to Laban: 'What is this thou hast done unto me? did not I serve with thee for Rachel? wherefore then hast thou beguiled me?' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:26</b></span> And Laban said: 'It is not so done in our place, to give the younger before the first-born. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:27</b></span> Fulfil the week of this one, and we will give thee the other also for the service which thou shalt serve with me yet seven other years.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:28</b></span> And Jacob did so, and fulfilled her week; and he gave him Rachel his daughter to wife. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:29</b></span> And Laban gave to Rachel his daughter Bilhah his handmaid to be her handmaid. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:30</b></span> And he went in also unto Rachel, and he loved also Rachel more than Leah, and served with him yet seven other years.</p>

                                <p class="torah"><span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:31</b></span> And the LORD saw that Leah was hated, and he opened her womb; but Rachel was barren. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:32</b></span> And Leah conceived, and bore a son, <span class="horizontal1"><b>and she called his name Reuben</b></span>; for she said: 'Because the LORD hath looked upon my affliction; for now my husband will love me.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:33</b></span> And she conceived again, and bore a son; and said: 'Because the LORD hath heard that I am hated, He hath therefore given me this son also.' <span class="horizontal1"><b>And she called his name Simeon</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:34</b></span> And she conceived again, and bore a son; and said: 'Now this time will my husband be joined unto me, because I have borne him three sons.' Therefore <span class="horizontal1"><b>was his name called Levi</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>29:35</b></span> And she conceived again, and bore a son; and she said: 'This time will I praise the LORD.' Therefore <span class="horizontal1"><b>she called his name Judah</b></span>; and she left off bearing. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:1</b></span> And when Rachel saw that she bore Jacob no children, Rachel envied her sister; and she said unto Jacob: 'Give me children, or else I die.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:2</b></span> And Jacob's anger was kindled against Rachel; and he said: 'Am I in God's stead, who hath withheld from thee the fruit of the womb?' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:3</b></span> And she said: 'Behold my maid Bilhah, go in unto her; that she may bear upon my knees, and I also may be builded up through her.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:4</b></span> And she gave him Bilhah her handmaid to wife; and Jacob went in unto her. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:5</b></span> And Bilhah conceived, and bore Jacob a son. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:6</b></span> And Rachel said: 'God hath judged me, and hath also heard my voice, and hath given me a son.' Therefore <span class="horizontal1"><b>called she his name Dan</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:7</b></span> And Bilhah Rachel's handmaid conceived again, and bore Jacob a second son. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:8</b></span> And Rachel said: 'With mighty wrestlings have I wrestled with my sister, and have prevailed.' <span class="horizontal1"><b>And she called his name Naphtali</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:9</b></span> When Leah saw that she had left off bearing, she took Zilpah her handmaid, and gave her to Jacob to wife. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:10</b></span> And Zilpah Leah's handmaid bore Jacob a son. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:11</b></span> And Leah said: 'Fortune is come!' <span class="horizontal1"><b>And she called his name Gad</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:12</b></span> And Zilpah Leah's handmaid bore Jacob a second son. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:13</b></span> And Leah said: 'Happy am I! for the daughters will call me happy.' <span class="horizontal1"><b>And she called his name Asher</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:14</b></span> And Reuben went in the days of wheat harvest, and found mandrakes in the field, and brought them unto his mother Leah. Then Rachel said to Leah: 'Give me, I pray thee, of thy son's mandrakes.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:15</b></span> And she said unto her: 'Is it a small matter that thou hast taken away my husband? and wouldest thou take away my son's mandrakes also?' And Rachel said: 'Therefore he shall lie with thee to-night for thy son's mandrakes.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:16</b></span> And Jacob came from the field in the evening, and Leah went out to meet him, and said: 'Thou must come in unto me; for I have surely hired thee with my son's mandrakes.' And he lay with her that night. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:17</b></span> And God hearkened unto Leah, and she conceived, and bore Jacob a fifth son. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:18</b></span> And Leah said: 'God hath given me my hire, because I gave my handmaid to my husband. <span class="horizontal1"><b>And she called his name Issachar</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:19</b></span> And Leah conceived again, and bore a sixth son to Jacob. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:20</b></span> And Leah said: 'God hath endowed me with a good dowry; now will my husband dwell with me, because I have borne him six sons.' <span class="horizontal1"><b>And she called his name Zebulun</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:21</b></span> <span class="horizontal1"><b>And afterwards she bore a daughter, and called her name Dinah</b></span>. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:22</b></span> And God remembered Rachel, and God hearkened to her, and opened her womb. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:23</b></span> And she conceived, and bore a son, and said: 'God hath taken away my reproach.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:24</b></span> <span class="horizontal1"><b>And she called his name Joseph</b></span>, saying: 'The LORD add to me another son.'</p>

                                <p class="torah"><span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:25</b></span> And it came to pass, when Rachel had borne Joseph, that Jacob said unto Laban: 'Send me away, that I may go unto mine own place, and to my country. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:26</b></span> Give me my wives and my children for whom I have served thee, and let me go; for thou knowest my service wherewith I have served thee.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:27</b></span> And Laban said unto him: 'If now I have found favour in thine eyes--I have observed the signs, and the LORD hath blessed me for thy sake.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:28</b></span> And he said: 'Appoint me thy wages, and I will give it.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:29</b></span> And he said unto him: 'Thou knowest how I have served thee, and how thy cattle have fared with me. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:30</b></span> For it was little which thou hadst before I came, and it hath increased abundantly; and the LORD hath blessed thee whithersoever I turned. And now when shall I provide for mine own house also?' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:31</b></span> And he said: 'What shall I give thee?' And Jacob said: 'Thou shalt not give me aught; if thou wilt do this thing for me, I will again feed thy flock and keep it. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:32</b></span> I will pass through all thy flock to-day, removing from thence every speckled and spotted one, and every dark one among the sheep, and the spotted and speckled among the goats; and of such shall be my hire. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:33</b></span> So shall my righteousness witness against me hereafter, when thou shalt come to look over my hire that is before thee: every one that is not speckled and spotted among the goats, and dark among the sheep, that if found with me shall be counted stolen.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:34</b></span> And Laban said: 'Behold, would it might be according to thy word.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:35</b></span> And he removed that day the he-goats that were streaked and spotted, and all the she-goats that were speckled and spotted, every one that had white in it, and all the dark ones among the sheep, and gave them into the hand of his sons. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:36</b></span> And he set three days' journey betwixt himself and Jacob. And Jacob fed the rest of Laban's flocks. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:37</b></span> And Jacob took him rods of fresh poplar, and of the almond and of the plane-tree; and peeled white streaks in them, making the white appear which was in the rods. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:38</b></span> And he set the rods which he had peeled over against the flocks in the gutters in the watering-troughs where the flocks came to drink; and they conceived when they came to drink. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:39</b></span> And the flocks conceived at the sight of the rods, and the flocks brought forth streaked, speckled, and spotted. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:40</b></span> And Jacob separated the lambs--he also set the faces of the flocks toward the streaked and all the dark in the flock of Laban--and put his own droves apart, and put them not unto Laban's flock. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:41</b></span> And it came to pass, whensoever the stronger of the flock did conceive, that Jacob laid the rods before the eyes of the flock in the gutters, that they might conceive among the rods; <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:42</b></span> but when the flock were feeble, he put them not in; so the feebler were Laban's, and the stronger Jacob's. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>30:43</b></span> And the man increased exceedingly, and had large flocks, and maid-servants and men-servants, and camels and asses.</p>

and I will be with thee.' <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:4</b></span> And Jacob sent and called Rachel and Leah to the field unto his flock, <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:5</b></span> and said unto them: 'I see your father's countenance, that it is not toward me as beforetime; but the God of my father hath been with me. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:6</b></span> And ye know that with all my power I have served your father. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:7</b></span> And your father hath mocked me, and changed my wages ten times; but God suffered him not to hurt me. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:8</b></span> If he said thus: The speckled shall be thy wages; then all the flock bore speckled; and if he said thus: The streaked shall be thy wages; then bore all the flock streaked. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:9</b></span> Thus God hath taken away the cattle of your father, and given them to me. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:10</b></span> And it came to pass at the time that the flock conceived, that I lifted up mine eyes, and saw in a dream, and, behold, the he-goats which leaped upon the flock were streaked, speckled, and grizzled. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:11</b></span> And the angel of God said unto me in the dream: Jacob; and I said: Here am I. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:12</b></span> And he said: Lift up now thine eyes, and see, all the he-goats which leap upon the flock are streaked, speckled, and grizzled; for I have seen all that Laban doeth unto thee. <span class="VerseNumber"><b>31:13</b></span> I am the God of Beth-el, where thou didst anoint a pillar, where thou didst vow a vow unto Me.
