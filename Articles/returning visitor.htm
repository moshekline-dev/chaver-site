It has been about five years since I decided to publish the results of my research on my own web site. At that time I envisioned the site primarily as a repository for my text of the Mishnah, an alternative to hardcopy publishing. The first articles I added were intended to demonstrate the value of visualizing the text in tabular form. My research took on a new direction in the last few years as I concentrated on the legal codes of the Torah. I have now completed the analysis of the three major codes. The Exodus code appears in Hebrew on the site, and Leviticus both in English and Hebrew. I have not yet uploaded Deuteronomy 12-26, although the analysis is finished. It is now quite definite that the tabular format of chapters of Mishnah was based on the format of the legal codes of the Torah. Not only are the formats identical, but the “weights” of the parts are too. By “weights” I refer to rhetorical devices such as the relative values of the columns. Both the Torah and the Mishnah distinguish between right and left columns in a manner akin to the Kabalistic values of right and left. In my article on the first chapter of Avot I refer to these values as the static rule and the Maharal of Prague relates to them as “love and fear.” The “inner” texts of the Torah and the Mishnah were encoded in the structure. Since the Torah preceded the Mishnah by over a thousand years, we have an indication of an esoteric literary tradition that spanned centuries.

While there may be a number of dissertations that can be written on the relationships between the texts, my primary concern is not academic. I believe that the approach I have taken can significantly add to the experience of studying the more difficult sections of the Torah as well as all of the Mishnah. It allows the reader to “see” the text with an overview that connects the parts. Moreover, it makes available the underlying philosophic meaning embedded in the arrangement of the laws. Stay tuned.
