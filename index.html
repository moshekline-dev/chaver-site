A Discovery in Biblical Literature

The Torah and Mishnah were composed as two-dimensional literary matrices—"woven texts"—with patterns that become visible only when the text is laid out in tabular format. This site presents four decades of research revealing the Torah's woven literary architecture hidden within these foundational texts.

As illustrated above, the Woven Torah reveals a second, "esoteric" structure hidden within the standard linear text—transforming a historical narrative into a timeless architectural map.

Experience It Yourself: Genesis 1

Toggle between the familiar linear reading and the woven structure to see the creation narrative transform. The same two-column pattern from the Decalogue appears here: Days 1–3 create the elements ex nihilo, while Days 4–6 animate them with progressive degrees of freedom.

Linear Mode

Genesis 1:1-2:3 (Standard Reading)

1:1-2 In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth. Now the earth was unformed and void, and darkness was upon the face of the deep; and the spirit of God hovered over the face of the waters.

1:3-5 And God said: 'Let there be light.' And there was light. And God saw the light, that it was good; and God divided the light from the darkness. And God called the light Day, and the darkness He called Night. And there was evening and there was morning, one day.

1:6-8 And God said: 'Let there be a firmament in the midst of the waters, and let it divide the waters from the waters.' And God made the firmament, and divided the waters which were under the firmament from the waters which were above the firmament; and it was so. And God called the firmament Heaven. And there was evening and there was morning, a second day.

1:9-13 And God said: 'Let the waters under the heaven be gathered together unto one place, and let the dry land appear.' And it was so. And God called the dry land Earth; and the gathering together of the waters called He Seas; and God saw that it was good. And God said: 'Let the earth put forth grass, herb yielding seed, and fruit-tree bearing fruit after its kind, wherein is the seed thereof, upon the earth.' And it was so... and God saw that it was good. And there was evening and there was morning, a third day.

1:14-19 And God said: 'Let there be lights in the firmament of the heaven to divide the day from the night; and let them be for signs, and for seasons, and for days and years...' And God made the two great lights: the greater light to rule the day, and the lesser light to rule the night; and the stars... and God saw that it was good. And there was evening and there was morning, a fourth day.

1:20-23 And God said: 'Let the waters swarm with swarms of living creatures, and let fowl fly above the earth in the open firmament of heaven.' And God created the great sea-monsters, and every living creature that creepeth... and God saw that it was good. And God blessed them, saying: 'Be fruitful, and multiply...' And there was evening and there was morning, a fifth day.

1:24-31 And God said: 'Let the earth bring forth the living creature after its kind...' And it was so... And God said: 'Let us make man in our image...' And God created man in His own image... And God blessed them... [continues through blessing and sixth day]

2:1-3 And the heaven and the earth were finished, and all the host of them. And on the seventh day God finished His work which He had made; and He rested on the seventh day from all His work which He had made. And God blessed the seventh day, and hallowed it; because that in it He rested from all his work which God in creating had made.

What the Woven Structure Reveals

  • Opening Chiasm (Row 1): The chiastic structure—Elohim→heaven→earth // earth→deep→spirit—demonstrates the author using literary form to communicate meaning.
  • Transfer of Divine Motion (Days 4–6): The right column shows progressive transfer of divine motion into creation with increasing degrees of freedom.
  • Four Classical Elements (Days 1–3): The left column creates Fire (light), Air (firmament), Water (seas), Earth (land).
  • Movement from Immanence to Transcendence: Row 1 shows deity inseparable from creation; Row 3 shows deity now separated (transcendent).

Frequently Asked Questions

What are woven texts?
Woven texts are biblical compositions organized as two-dimensional literary matrices rather than purely linear narratives. When the Torah and Mishnah are laid out in table format, they reveal sophisticated structural patterns with both horizontal (row) and vertical (column) relationships between passages.
How many units are in the Torah?
The Torah consists of 86 discrete literary units across the Five Books of Moses: 76 regular units and 10 irregular units. Each unit exhibits internal structural perfection and clear boundary markers.
Is this research peer-reviewed?
Yes. Moshe Kline's work has been published in prestigious peer-reviewed journals including the Journal of Biblical Literature (2025) and Journal of Hebrew Scriptures (2008).
