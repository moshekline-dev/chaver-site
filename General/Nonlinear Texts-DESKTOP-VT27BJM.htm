NonLinear Texts

The Torah and the Mishnah are nonlinear texts. This means that the flow of ideas does not necessarily follow the flow of the laws or narrative. The ideas are presented in blocks and the flow is from block to block.

Blocks of text

A block of text can be a single verse or a group of related verses. The technical term for a block of verses is "pericope." The flow of a linear text from pericope to pericope can be visualized like this:

P1>P2>P3>P4>P5>P6etc.        

But not all of our texts are linear. Sometimes they are organized in pairs or triads of pericopes and the conceptual flow is from pair to pair:

P1P2>P3P4>P5P6>P7P8etc.        

When we try to read a nonlinear text like the mishnah as a linear text, we are struck by the "jumps" between parts of the text, like between P2 and P3 above. That is because the similarity between pericopes P1 and P2 created an expectation for another similar pericope. However, the text was actually composed in such a way that P1 and P2 form a dyad. The conceptual flow is from dyad to dyad.

P1+P2>P3+P4>P5+P6>P7+P8etc.            

Each pair of pericopes has a common element. The conceptual flow is from common element to common element.
